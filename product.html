CO-EXTRUDED PROFILES

ASP Co-extruded profiles are made of two different compounds using solid EPDM and sponge EPDM with metal inserts. These profiles have varied applications where water, dust, and noise have to be kept out, such as in automobiles, industries, and canopies.

WIND SHIELDS / SOLID RUBBER / BUILDING PROFILE

Solid profiles are often known as EPDM. Dense rubber profiles are made in different hardness as per customer specifications and are used for the following applications:

    1.Wind shields and fixed window panels
    2.Glazing rubbers for bus and truck body building
    3.Glazing rubber for container sealing, railways, shipping applications, and others

SPONGE PROFILE

ASP cellular door profiles fit snugly into the hold of the auto body. With excellent compressibility, they are the best sealants for doors. These profiles provide better tightness and effectively keep air, dust, and water out. They also ensure a smooth closing of the doors, and when the vehicle runs, their anti-vibration quality ensures a silent drive.

FLOCKED GLASS RUN CHANNELS

These are guides for windows which can be raised and lowered. They come in various sizes, must be functional and hard-wearing, though subject to severe conditions such as vibration, climate, and aerodynamic effects. ASP channels feature excellent air and water tightness even when the window is lowered or raised, which is very important in curved windows, which are much used in today's motor vehicles.

STICKING PROFILE

These profiles are designed for application on vehicle bodies, specifically around doors and luggage compartments, to effectively keep out dust and water. Typically made from high-quality synthetic materials, the rubber tubes, often in sponge rubber form, provide the durability and elasticity required for long-lasting performance. These rubber profiles are technologically bonded to a PVC profile that incorporates a metal insert, allowing for quick and easy fitting to the vehicle body.
